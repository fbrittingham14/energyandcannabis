Your Cannabis Isn’t As Green As You Think

Why sun-grown weed is better for the planet and your conscience

Cannabis is the poster child of the back-to-land movement, grown and sold by nature-loving hippies… right?
Today’s cannabis industry tells a different story. Post-legalization, large indoor grow operations backed by VC funding have flooded the market, a far cry from the small family farms of Humboldt County.
Now the majority of cannabis sold in dispensaries in the US is grown indoors. Although growing indoors leads to a more consistent product, it contributes to significantly more greenhouse gas emissions. Behind the green packaging and eco-chic branding lies a carbon-intensive supply chain.
Growing cannabis indoors emits 7x more greenhouse gases per kilogram than growing cannabis outdoors.

How Bad Is It, Really?

Let’s put it in context…

The greenhouse gas emissions released from producing one 1-gram joint of cannabis grown outdoors is equivalent to driving a car 2 miles, but that same joint filled with cannabis grown indoors releases the same amount of emissions as driving a car 12 miles - and we aren’t talking about hybrid cars!

Why does indoor cultivated cannabis have such high emissions?

Indoor cannabis grow operations are essentially small factories using precise farming methods. To maximize yield, they use:

  • High-intensity lights
  • Air conditioners
  • Dehumidifiers
  • Fans
  • Water pumps

Put image of indoor grow here

These practices require A LOT of energy, like electricity and natural gas. Producing the amount of energy these factories need emits a lot of greenhouse gases. Their PG&E bills are through the roof.

Per square foot in a year, an indoor cannabis factory will use 40 times the power of a hospital operating room and 100 times the power of a typical Walmart. That’s still less than a typical AWS data center, but it’s still HUGE.

Why Are They Growing Indoors at All?

Indoor grows offer consistency, higher THC levels, and more harvest cycles per year, but they come with major environmental costs.

Consistency, strength, and yields.

Consistency

Consumers have come to expect that every time they buy a specific product, it will give them the same high each time, just like we expect a Hershey chocolate bar to taste the same every time we buy one. When cannabis is grown outdoors, it is subject to varying weather and soil chemistry that could cause the makeup of the cannabis to vary from crop to crop.

Strength

The environmental control of indoor factories can lead to higher THC levels. At these levels though, it’s mostly a marketing ploy as studies show that the impact of increasing TCH levels on a consumer’s experience plateaus at levels above that of high THC outdoor-grown cannabis.

Yields

In these controlled environments, indoor cannabis can be harvested 5-6 times per year, leading to much higher yields than typical outdoor farms that can only harvest 1 or 2 times per year. However, supply has started to outpace demand and there is a lot of wasted cannabis each year. It makes us wonder whether the industry needs 5-6 harvests each year…

If Energy Is the Problem, Can’t They Just Use Solar Panels?

It’s a good question—and one that many growers have asked. A Santa Barbara cultivator found they would need 25x the area of their roof to install enough solar panels to power their indoor grow. That’s just not feasible for most operations. And, it’s a lot of land.

Is There Anything Else That Could Make Growing Cannabis More Sustainable?

There are sustainability upgrades like composting waste, switching to LED lights, or growing lower-energy strains — but none have the impact of simply growing outdoors.

What You Can Do

Everyone

Ultimately, legalizing cannabis federally would allow for interstate commerce. This means states that had the ability to grow outdoors would be able to sell their sun-grown cannabis to places where weather doesn’t allow. Write to your US Senators and Representatives to tell them you support legalizing cannabis federally.

California Residents

Ask your dispensary for sun-grown cannabis. Some farms even label their products as such—support them with your dollars.

Residents of other states where cannabis is legal

It’s tougher. But you can still make a difference. Talk to your dispensary, ask about growing practices, and let your elected representatives know you care about sustainable cannabis.
