Your Cannabis Isn’t As Green As You Think

Why sun-grown weed is better for the planet and your conscience

Where Are These Emissions Coming From?

Indoor cannabis cultivation has become the dominant method since legalization, driving industry emissions through the roof.

How Bad Is It, Really?

Producing one 1-gram joint indoors releases the same emissions as driving a car 12 miles, compared to just 2 miles for outdoor-grown cannabis.

Why Are They Growing Indoors at All?

Indoor grows offer consistency, higher THC levels, and more harvest cycles per year, but they come with major environmental costs.

If Energy Is the Problem, Can’t They Just Use Solar Panels?

Even with 100% solar power, indoor grows can’t decrease their emissions to the level of outdoor cultivation. Outdoor cannabis benefits from natural sunlight — no need to burn fossil fuels to mimic nature.

Is There Anything Else That Could Make Growing Cannabis More Sustainable?

There are sustainability upgrades like composting waste, switching to LED lights, or growing lower-energy strains — but none have the impact of simply growing outdoors, which reduces emissions far more than all other improvements combined.

What About Land and Water Needs for Outdoor Grows?

Outdoor grows use more land and water than indoor ones — roughly 5x the land and 3x the water — but indoor grows consume 61x more power, making energy the largest environmental cost.

What You Can Do

Support sun-grown cannabis by asking your dispensary about their sourcing, advocating for sustainable practices, and encouraging federal legalization to allow interstate commerce of outdoor-grown products.
