Your Cannabis Isn’t As Green As You Think

Why sun-grown weed is better for the planet and your conscience

Growing cannabis indoors emits 6.5x
more greenhouse gases per kilogram than growing cannabis outdoors.

Cannabis is often seen as a natural, eco-friendly product, but the reality is more complex.
Today, most legal cannabis is grown indoors in energy-intensive facilities, a far cry from the small, sustainable farms of California’s Golden Triangle. These indoor grow operations are a major source of climate pollution.

How Bad Is It, Really?

Let’s put it in context…

Producing a 1-gram joint indoors emits as much as driving 12 miles, compared to 2 miles for outdoor-grown cannabis.

The greenhouse gas emissions released from producing one 1-gram joint are equivalent to the emissions released from producing 321 cigarettes!

Why does indoor cultivated cannabis have such high emissions?

Indoor cannabis grow operations use energy-intensive precision farming to maximize yield:

Generating the amount of energy these facilities need emits substantial greenhouse gas emissions. Per square foot in a year, an indoor cannabis factory will use 8,540 kbtu/sf-y which is...

40x the power of a hospital operating room.

100x the power of a typical Walmart.

Behind the green packaging and eco-chic branding lies an unnecessarily carbon-intensive industry.

Why Are They Growing Indoors at All?

That begs the question: if their PG&E bills are so high, why are they growing indoors at all?

Consistency

Just as consumers expect the same taste from each Hershey bar, they expect a consistent high from a given product. But outdoor grown cannabis can vary by crop due to changing weather and soil.

Strength

Indoor grows can boost THC levels, but studies show effects plateau above levels already reached by high THC outdoor cannabis, making the extra potency more marketing than meaningful.

Harvests

Indoor cannabis yields 5–6 harvests per year, far more than the 1–2 from outdoor farms. But with supply now outpacing demand, a lot goes to waste. Does the industry need so many harvests?

If Energy Is the Problem, Can’t They Just Use Solar Panels?

Is There Anything Else That Could Make Growing Cannabis More Sustainable?

There are sustainability upgrades like composting waste, switching to LED lights, or growing lower-energy strains, but none have the impact of one simple change:

Growing cannabis outdoors.

Emissions of today's cannabis industry:

100% outdoor cultivation would lead to a 75% decrease in emissions.

100% solar + electrification would lead to a 50% decrease in emissions.

Shifting indoor to greenhouse would lead to a 25% decrease in emissions.

Electrification with LEDs + heat pumps would lead to a 10% decrease in emissions.

Composting plant waste would lead to a 2% decrease in emissions.

Credit: Cannabis industry by fran4good

*Note: these options are NOT additive.

Indoor grows still can’t decrease their emissions to the level of outdoor cultivation. No need to burn fossil fuels when you have the sun (sun emoji?)!

What You Can Do

Everyone

Ultimately, legalizing cannabis federally would allow for interstate commerce. This means states that had the ability to grow outdoors would be able to sell their sun-grown cannabis to places where weather doesn’t allow. Write to your US Senators and Representatives to tell them you support legalizing cannabis federally.

California Residents

Ask your dispensary for sun-grown cannabis. Some farms even label their products as such—support them with your dollars.

Residents of other states where cannabis is legal

It’s tougher. But you can still make a difference. Talk to your dispensary, ask about growing practices, and let your elected representatives know you care about sustainable cannabis.
